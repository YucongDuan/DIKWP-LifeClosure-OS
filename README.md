# DIKWP LifeClosure OS

**Recursive Viability Closure (RVC)** is an operational, falsifiable solution to the definition-of-life problem.

## Final operational definition

At a preregistered spatial scale, time window and environment, a candidate system is alive when five functions form an intervention-supported strongly connected causal loop:

1. **C1 Individuation:** the system produces, maintains and repairs the selective boundary or constraints that individuate it.
2. **C2 Work closure:** it captures free energy and matter and directs work toward rebuilding its own constraints.
3. **C3 Embodied meaning:** environmental differences change action because they have consequences for the system's own viability, with history physically changing future response.
4. **C4 Endogenous normativity:** the organization generates its own viable/non-viable distinctions and corrective action.
5. **C5 Reflexive rule revision:** the system can modify at least some of the rules, mappings or control constraints by which it adapts.

A **living lineage** additionally reconstructs C1-C5 across generations with heritable variation and expansion of its possibility space.

The binary question is whether this causal closure exists. The continuous question is how robust, deep, broad and recoverable it is.

## Package principle

The package terminates unproductive lexical debate by converting every disagreement into one of five objects: a registered boundary, a causal edge, an experiment, a counterexample or a Kill Condition. “Final” therefore means a closed and falsifiable operating protocol, not immunity from revision by stronger evidence.

## Safety boundary

Illustrative classifications and scores are research priors. Work involving cells, synthetic organisms, environmental release, clinical material, autonomous hardware or astrobiology requires independent ethics, biosafety, security and planetary-protection review.
